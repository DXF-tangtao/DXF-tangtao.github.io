Why China Feels Like The Future: 4 Cyberpunk Cities Every Traveler Should Visit

为何中国充满未来感：每位旅行者都该造访的4座赛博朋克之城

A local Chinese perspective on futuristic cities, AI-powered travel, hidden culture, and the side of China most foreigners never see.
以中国人本土视角，带你领略未来都市、人工智能赋能旅行、小众人文底蕴，看见大多数外国人从未了解过的真实中国。

Before You Read

阅读前言

Most international content about China feels strangely outdated.
海外大部分关于中国的内容，都莫名停留在过时的印象里。

Some people still imagine China as a place full of bicycles, cash payments, crowded streets, and old stereotypes from 20 years ago.
仍有不少人对中国的印象，还停留在满街自行车、现金交易、街巷拥挤，固守着二十年前的老旧刻板印象。

But the real China in 2026 feels completely different.
但2026年的真实中国，早已是另一番模样。

Today, mobile payment is everywhere. High-speed rail connects nearly every major city. AI tools are deeply integrated into daily life. Delivery robots move through shopping malls. Hotels use robot assistants. Facial recognition opens office buildings. Even small fruit vendors on the street often accept digital payments.
如今，移动支付普及大街小巷，高铁贯通国内各大城市，人工智能深度融入日常生活。商场里配送机器人往来穿梭，酒店配备智能机器人管家，写字楼靠人脸识别门禁通行，就连街边小摊小贩也普遍支持数字支付。

As someone born and raised in Chongqing, China, I’ve watched this transformation happen in real time.
我土生土长在重庆，亲眼见证了这场翻天覆地的变迁。

That’s why I created this website.
这也是我搭建这个博客的初衷。

Not to show a “perfect” version of China.
我并非要塑造一个“完美无瑕”的中国形象。

But to share a more authentic one.
只是想分享最真实的中国面貌。

A version beyond headlines, stereotypes, and tourist brochures.
跳出新闻头条、刻板偏见与旅游宣传册的固有框架。

This article combines two things I care about most:
这篇文章融合了我最关注的两大主题：

  • China’s futuristic cities
  • China’s rapidly growing AI and robotics culture
  • 充满未来感的中国城市
  • 飞速崛起的中国人工智能与机器人文化

If you’ve ever wondered what it actually feels like to travel through modern China, this guide is for you.
如果你好奇游历现代中国究竟是何种体验，这份指南正适合你。

Part 1 — 4 Chinese Cities That Feel Like The Future

第一部分 — 四座自带未来质感的中国城市

1. Chongqing — China’s Real Cyberpunk City

1. 重庆 — 中国真正的赛博朋克之城

The first time I saw foreigners describe Chongqing as “Cyberpunk City,” I laughed.
第一次看到外国人把重庆称作“赛博朋克之城”时，我只觉得好笑。

Because for locals, this chaos is normal.
因为在本地人眼里，这种魔幻错落本就是日常。

But after traveling outside Chongqing, I realized something:
可走出重庆游历各地后，我才恍然发现：

This city genuinely looks unreal.
这座城市的风貌，确实美得不像现实人间。

Roads overlap on top of skyscrapers.
高楼之上架立交，道路层层叠叠纵横交错。

Apartment buildings climb mountains.
居民楼宇依山而建，顺着山势蜿蜒向上。

Monorails pass directly through residential buildings.
单轨列车穿楼而过，成为独一份的城市奇观。

At night, the entire city glows orange and neon like a scene from Blade Runner.
夜幕降临，整座城市橘色灯火与霓虹交映，宛如《银翼杀手》里的未来场景。

Chongqing isn’t just visually crazy — it also feels deeply alive.
重庆不止视觉上充满魔幻，更有着浓郁的人间烟火气。

You’ll see elderly people playing mahjong beside futuristic shopping malls. Hot pot restaurants stay open until 3 AM. Tiny alleyways hide some of the best food you’ll ever eat.
新潮商场旁，老人围坐搓麻将；火锅店营业至凌晨三点；幽深小巷里藏着无数人间美味。

And somehow, it all works.
繁华与市井奇妙相融，浑然自成一派。

现代科技与闲适生活完美平衡，正是这座城市的独特魅力。

1. Humanoid Robot Half Marathon in Beijing

1. 北京人形机器人半程马拉松赛事

On April 19, 2026, the Beijing Yizhuang Half Marathon and Humanoid Robot Half Marathon officially began, creating a spectacular event that combined sports, artificial intelligence, and futuristic technology.

2026 年 4 月 19 日，北京亦庄城市半程马拉松暨人形机器人半程马拉松赛事正式开赛，打造了一场融合体育运动、人工智能与未来科技的盛大盛会。

The race quickly attracted global attention and became one of the most talked-about tech events in China this year.

这场赛事迅速吸引了全球目光，也成为本年度中国最受热议的科技盛事之一。

What made this competition truly incredible was how advanced the humanoid robots had become.

这场赛事最令人惊叹的，是人形机器人如今已达到的超高技术水准。

Compared with previous years, the robots could run faster, maintain better balance, and operate for much longer distances without stopping.

相比往年，机器人奔跑速度更快、平衡能力更强，且能不间断完成更长距离的行进。

Some robots even completed the race at speeds nearly seven minutes faster than the human half-marathon world record.

部分机器人完赛速度，甚至比人类半程马拉松世界纪录还要快近 7 分钟。

More than 100 humanoid robot teams from 11 provinces across China participated in the event, running alongside over 12,000 human runners on the same course.

来自国内 11 个省市的 100 余支人形机器人战队参赛，与 12000 余名人类跑者同场赛道开跑。

Nearly 40% of the robots used fully autonomous navigation systems, allowing them to avoid obstacles, adjust their pace, and complete the race with minimal human assistance.

近 40% 的参赛机器人搭载全自主导航系统，可自主避障、实时调整步频节奏，几乎无需人工干预即可全程完赛。

The event was not only a showcase of Chinese robotics development, but also an international technology gathering.

这场赛事不仅是中国机器人产业实力的一次集中展示，更是一场国际性科技盛会。

Outstanding teams from Germany, France, Portugal, Brazil, and several other countries also joined the competition, turning the marathon into a truly global stage for humanoid robot innovation.

来自德国、法国、葡萄牙、巴西等多国的优秀战队也同台参赛，让这场马拉松成为全球人形机器人技术创新的国际舞台。

For many foreign viewers, the race felt like something straight out of a sci-fi movie.

在许多外国观众眼中，这场赛事宛如科幻电影照进现实。

But in China, this kind of technology is increasingly becoming part of real life.

而在中国，这类前沿科技正一步步走入日常、融入生活。

From AI-powered transportation systems to robot services in hotels and restaurants, the future is arriving faster than most people expect.

从人工智能智慧交通，到酒店餐厅的机器人服务，未来生活到来的速度，远超大多数人的想象。

The Beijing Humanoid Robot Half Marathon is more than just a race — it is a glimpse into how robotics, AI, and human society may evolve together in the near future.

北京人形机器人半程马拉松，早已不只是一场赛事；它让世人提前窥见：在不远的未来，机器人、人工智能与人类社会将如何共生共进、协同发展。

Yes.

In Beijing’s Yizhuang district, humanoid robots competed in a half marathon using autonomous navigation systems.

Some robots could:

  • 自主规避道路障碍
  • Adjust movement in real time
  • 实时调整行走姿态
  • Navigate independently

  • 长距离行走保持身体平衡

But it’s real.

大多数人从未见过的真实中国
